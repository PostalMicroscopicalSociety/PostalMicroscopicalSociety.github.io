Springtail of the Month - 4
 Continuation sheet 3

Organs and Setae Details (continued):



This is an enlarged part of the posterior end from which the ratio (Length of long posterior setae)/(Length of mucro) can be calculated with reasonable accuracy. Measure two mucros and four setae, calculate averages, and divide. Do the red markers help? Gisin listed 3 closely similar species with the following variations of ratio: F.manolachei Bagnall 2; F.nana Gisin 2.3 to 3.0; F.quadrioculata (Tullberg) 3.5 to 5.




All 3 species above share similar setae on the anterior surfaces of their manubrium and dentes. They have 1 + 1 strong setae near the dental end of the manubrium, and 6 - 8 setae on each dens. The anterior surface of the furca becomes uppermost (dorsal) when it is folded away (see illustration).


These setae are difficult to get in profile. They can be checked by focussing up and down on transparent mounts of the furca either folded or extended. The 1 + 1 thick setae on the manubrial anterior surface are unsharp compared with the 15 thinner ones on its posterior surface.This is a good example of problems with the UK springtails. We need lots of collections and culturing to form opinions on whether all 3 are good species.
