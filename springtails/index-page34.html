Springtail of the Month - 3
 Continuation sheet 2

To arrive at species brittanica in the keys by Gisin and Fjellberg:
Dens with 3 setae; mucro with 2 teeth; tenaculum with 3 + 3 teeth and 1 seta; 2 oblique curved markings on the dens; 7 + 7 setae on the manubrium; ocelli 8 + 8, two of which are smaller. Our specimens clearly showed only 2 tenacular teeth on each side, but the curved markings, presumed not present in the closely related T.wahlgreni (Linnaniemi 1907) are considered to be specific for brittanica. T.wahlgreni, however, is listed as having 2 + 2 tenacular teeth.
Because the furca is reduced and it could not be extended without damage, it was impossible to photograph clearly the setae on manubrium. They matched the numbers given above after careful focusing up and down.
