COOL PIXELS (7)

by Carel Sartory

[Balsam Post Issue 82 (January 2009) page 11 to 19 continuation sheet 2.]


Note: for completeness please refer to the original article.


Spike Walker's Illumitran conversion at the Dale Fort plankton microscopy weekend: It has been converted into a transmitted light flash macro setup using a Zeiss Tessovar and a simple stage and condenser system.
(Additional image - not in BP).
Bowens Illumitran purchased on eBay: Note the carriers for holding different sizes of mounted transparencies up to 60 x 70mm and for strips of 35mm and medium format roll film.
(Fig.10 in BP).
The lower part of the 35mm film strip holder used to support a 3” x 1” microscope slide of a large stained plant section.
(Fig.11 in BP).
The Flash box of the Illumitran showing the control panel, the brightness control knob and the diffuser/filter tray partially withdrawn. Note the Wein Safe-Sync HSHS Voltage Regulator hot shoe device attached to the end of the flash trigger cable. It is used to limit the flash trigger voltage. Never plug an Illumitran directly into a digital camera; it will seriously damage the camera's electronics.
(Fig.12 in BP).
To use an Illumitran with a Nikon Coolpix 4500 or similar camera, the Nikon AS-E900 remote flash cable needs to be used to attach the Safe-sync.
(Fig.13 in BP).
A Coolpix 4500 mounted on the Illumitran’s bellows using an adaptor to take a x10, 28mm threaded eyepiece screwed into the filter thread of the camera and a 60mm macro lens to photograph a microscope slide specimen. The Safe-sync can be seen to the left of the bellows.
(Fig.14 in BP).
A DSLR camera with a 50mm macro lens and the Illumitran with the bellows removed being used to photograph a microscope specimen.
(Fig.15 in BP).
