COOL PIXELS (7)

by Carel Sartory

[Balsam Post Issue 82 (January 2009) page 11 to 19 continuation sheet 3.]


Note: for completeness please refer to the original article.


The above set-up controlled from a laptop to photograph the poppy seed section shown below; computer control allows immediate review of the RAW images so that any lighting adjustments required can be made.
(Fig.16 in BP).
A transparency of Metridium senile, the Plumose anemone taken in the early 1970s; copied with the above set-up; a quicker and easier method than using a modern computer slide copier.
(Fig.17 in BP).
This image is from a very thin, old and brittle negative of my fathers study taken in the early 1960s.
(Fig.18 in BP).
The Illumitran is particularly useful for photographing large specimens like this longitudinal section through a poppy seed pod; the specimen is 30mm x 20mm in size.
(Additional image - not in BP).
A transverse section through a poppy seed pod; the specimen is 20mm x 18mm in size.
(Additional image - not in BP).
A longitudinal section through a Passion Flower, Passiiflora caerulea; the specimen is 27mm x 18mm in size.
(Fig.19 in BP).
A Watson whole mount slide of a dragonfly labelled Agrion pulchellum; the specimen is over 35mm long.
