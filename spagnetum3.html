MY FAVOURITE PLANT COMMUNITY - SPHAGNETUM

by Keith Brocklehurst

[Balsam Post Issue 61 (October 2003) page 42.]

Second continuation page.
20. Centropyxis spinosa. Possibly a variety of C. aculeata. Aperture distorted. Oral surface concave, aboral convex. Umbrella-shaped.
21. Lesquereusia spiralis. Test ± ovoid with neck leading to aperture. Cytoplasm dark, elongate curved siliceous rods support the test; unmistakable. Holding a desmid hemi-cell; has it fed?
22. Euglypha strigosa. Ovoid test ca.75 µm long, laterally compressed, aperture oval bordered by toothed aperture plates. Some 200-300 overlapping siliceous plates regularly arranged. Spines, attached between the plates, are easily dislodged. Aperture plates of several species found in tests of other species e.g. N. flabellulum, due, presumably due to predation.
23. Difflugia bacillifera. Test protected by agglutinated diatom frustules; new tests presumably made at fission, of small mineral particles at first. Test brittle and rather crushed at posterior end. Phase-contrast. Length 160 µm.
24. D. bacillifera.Another specimen, by bright field. Cytoplasm red, test undamaged.
24a. Hyalosphenia papilio. Transparent test without plates or particles and laterally compressed. Single spherical nucleus with nucleoli appearing dark under phase-contrast. Zoochlorellae present. Most amenable species to study live. Known to feed on rotifers.
25. Hyalosphenia elegans. Smaller species, flask-shaped with depressions in the expanded part.
26. Cyphoderia trochus. Retort-shaped test supported by imbricated silica plates Bulge of cytoplasm due to pressure. Normal pseudopodia are fine (filopodia).
27. Cyphoderia trochus. PVAL mount, cytoplasm withdrawn and shrunk, but newly secreted idiosomes detectable (bright white). Exquisite regular imbricated arrangement of plates and aperture clear.
28. Placocista spinosa. Ovoid test with prominent spines and regular rows of overlapping plates. Aperture is slit-like and to the left. Dorsoventrally flattened? But not observed live and moving.
29. Amphitrema wrightianum. Test colourless or yellow, ± ovoid, 70 µm long, with an aperture and collar at each end. Zoochlorellae present.
30. Nebela triangulata. Test rather transparent containing minute angular plates. Length ca.100µm.This specimen is similar to G.S,West’s specimens from Loch Shiel, Inverness. The specimen figured was collected by David Williamson from a northern Scottish Lochan.



31. Stentor coeruleus. A well-known Spirotrich ciliate. Often attached, sessile and trumpet-shaped but can contract up and swim away. >1 mm. long when extended. Macronucleus beaded.
32. Pyxidium sp. A stalked ciliate , stalks unbranched, attached to vegetation.
