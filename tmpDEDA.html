Fred Loxton, editor of the Balsam Post from 1992 to early 2002, was happy to take illustrations for the Newsletter on the following conditions: The images had to be in 1 month before publication; illustrations had to be in black and white (no grayscale) and without large expanses of black; drawings had to be capable of 70% reduction.

Fred's readiness to accept the illustrations I submitted played a key role in the development of my drawing skills and how I perceived microscopical subjects. Above, you can see not only a range of subjects taken from the PMS microscope slide collection or from articles submitted to the newsletter but also a breadth of techniques tried. From simple line drawings, through drawing from projection, visualising diatoms in three dimensions, reconstructive portraits, to interest in shape and framing.
