Clematis vitalba L. - Old Mans Beard

Part of a cross section through the stem of the clematis plant. The circular image contains mainly small ovals representing the cells in the stem. some of these have quite thick cell walls. Running diagonally down in one strand are several rows of longitudinally cut cells. Scattered throughout are the very large oval sections through the water carrying vascular cells. Balsam Post cover, issue No. 29, October 1995. This line rendering was drawn by projecting the microscope image onto a piece of paper in a darkened room and then tracing the outlines. Finer detail was then included from observation afterwards. From PMS box 94/25 by Ernie Ives.
