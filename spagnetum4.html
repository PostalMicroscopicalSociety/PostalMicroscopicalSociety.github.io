MY FAVOURITE PLANT COMMUNITY - SPHAGNETUM

by Keith Brocklehurst

[Balsam Post Issue 61 (October 2003) page 42.]

Third continuation page.
33. Paramecium bursaria. Another well-known ciliate because of its bright green zoochlorellae.Up to 150µm long.
34. Spirostomum teres. Elongate, cylindrical with longitudinal myonemes, highly flexible and contractile in organic debris but in water swims rapidly with cilia. Single ovoid macronucleus .Long canal leading back into the large posterior vacuole. Collected from Gordale Beck.
35. S. teres. Higher magnification showing anterior membranellae.
36. Ciliate. To be identified. Anterior zone of membranellae (azm), posterior vacuoles, central macronucleus.
37. Paramecium caudatum. Fed with congo red stained yeast cells to demonstrate acid phase of digestion (blue) in vacuoles. Tuft of rigid cilia at posterior end – hence specific name caudatum.
38. P.caudatum. Middle section. Macronucleus, food vacuoles, etc.
39. A Rhabdocoele. Probably Dalyellia. Exhibits ciliary and muscular movement. Seen between two sphagnum leaves.
40. Keratella. An empty lorica of this rotifer, species is serratula. Also a test of Arcella sp., etc.
41. Nauplius. The larva of a copepod. 3 pairs of appendages. Moves in jerks.
42. Chaetonotus sp. A gastrotrich. Exhibit gliding ciliary locomotion and muscular movements.
43. An oligochaete worm to be identified.
44. Sphagnum lawn.
