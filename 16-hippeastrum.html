Hippeastrum pollen

This image actually shows some anomalous behaviour of Hippeastrum pollen in 50% glycerol, a common solution in which to observe pollen. The oval pollen grain has a finely sculptured surface which has been split open in the larger image to partially reveal the pollen cell nucleus. The smaller images show the gradual swelling of the pollen grain until the cell contents escape the pollen coat - a totally unintended phenomenon. A better observation of the same pollen was observed in dilute sugar soltion, where the pollen remained intact and germinated pollen tubes. Balsam Post cover, issue No. 45, October 1999.
